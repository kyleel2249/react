Fortunately, or unfortunately, we don\u2019t get to choose our family members. As we let out our first cry as babies, we are surrounded by people to whom we give the biological right and ability to make us shed heart-wrenching tears for the rest of our lives. Let\u2019s say some family members explore this ability way too much and unapologetically so.<\/span><\/p>\n

Talking about apology, is it just me or family members just don\u2019t apologize? Family members can commit actions that should be punishable by law against you and just for the mere fact that you share some DNA, they think everything should be forgiven, forgotten and never spoken of. One particular set of people comes to mind, our parents! I won\u2019t even begin to mention ways our parents hurt us before it gets a bit too personal. (that\u2019s a topic for a different blog post). Anyway, sometimes I wish our parents would just stop playing God and acknowledge the fact that they are humans who can make mistakes too. We get that they care about us. We get that they want the best for us but thinking they owe us no apology for the pain they cause<\/i><\/a> just because they birthed us seems unfair. We always hear the quotation \u201cChildren obey your parents in the Lord, for this is right. Honour thy father and mother\u201d<\/em> but we barely even mention the second part of this that says \u201cFathers, do not provoke your children to anger\u201d<\/em>. Even God knows that this is a 2-way street so why do parents choose to ignore their bit?<\/p>\n

<\/a>
Parents expect their children to admit and apologize for their mistakes but when the roles are reversed, they suddenly turn mute. Sometimes, children even take the blame for situations that are clearly the parents\u2019 fault, but we\u2019re taught to just shut up and say sorry to our parents whether or not we are right. We seem to be fostering an \u201cAdults don\u2019t apologize\u201d<\/i><\/a> culture which we might end up passing on to our children. We must respect our parents no doubt. If for nothing at all, they at some point provided food for us to eat to say the least but should they be above reproach? I see people go about their lives carrying the burden of pain caused by parents. To think that a lot of family feuds would easily be resolved if parents were to say the words \u201cI was wrong, I\u2019m sorry\u201d<\/p>\n
