Winnie Dabi Diamond and her partner in business, Judith Asampana started a business right after their National Service with the idea whiles studying on campus to build up a business for themselves and then as part of contributing to society, employ others to control the unemployment rate and further boost the business.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n

The brain behind Nectar Juice Gh<\/u><\/b><\/h2>\n
In an interview with the co-founder of Nectar Juice Gh, Ms Dabi Diamond said the business will go a long way to survive and employ others to help control the unemployment rate in the country just as other business owners might help to do the same.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
Winnie Dabi Diamond is a young lady who graduated from the University of professional studies, Accra with a BSc. Business Administration (Degree), the co-founder of Nectar Juice Gh.<\/div>\n
Her partner in the Nectar juice business is Judith Asampana, a graduate of Lancaster University with a Law degree.<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
<\/a><\/td>\n<\/tr>\n
Winnie & Judith<\/td>\n<\/tr>\n<\/tbody>\n<\/table>\n

She is Ghanaian from the Volta region but has lived in Accra all her life. She is a very determined person, very goal oriented and really loves to push people to do things they have always wanted to do.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

\n
<\/a><\/div>\n

The Journey<\/u><\/b><\/h2>\n<\/div>\n
She noticed over the years that she’s a very handy person; I mean, I’m really good at doing things with her hands, a visual learner and learnt a lot through that as well as practical means. That is how she realized that she’s a “jack of all trades.”<\/p>\n

Over the years she started learning a lot of handy works that could easily bring money to the table. First was makeup, wig making and <\/u>then making of juices. Along the way, what came up next was a little buying and selling.\u00a0 Currently, her main focus is on the juice business although she does the others part-time.<\/p>\n

Taking Off<\/u><\/b><\/h2>\n<\/div>\n
The juice business started as a result of her latter days during service as the thought of not being retained at her place of service.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n
<\/a><\/td>\n<\/tr>\n
<\/td>\n<\/tr>\n<\/tbody>\n<\/table>\n
<\/div>\n

As a result, she started thinking of other means to sustain or support herself\u00a0right after National Service so she approached a very good friend of hers and pitched in the idea of both of us coming together to bring this dream into reality.<\/p>\n

<\/div>\n
<\/a><\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
Basically, at Nectar Juice, they produce fresh fruit juices for all occasions; weddings, naming ceremonies, parties, corporate meetings or events and many more. They also sell to individuals and sell at fairs as well.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
<\/a><\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
They look out for the best for our customers in regard to their health.\u00a0<\/span>Also, they<\/span>\u00a0make sure all their juices a naturally made with no added preservatives or any chemical, giving customers a wide range of flavours and tastes which is 100% natural.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n
<\/a><\/td>\n<\/tr>\n
Natural flavours<\/td>\n<\/tr>\n<\/tbody>\n<\/table>\n

\u00a0<\/a><\/p>\n

<\/div>\n
Some of the fruits used for the juices are pineapple, watermelon, mango, guava with a mixture of some vegetables such as beetroot, carrot, dandelion, etc.<\/div>\n
Recently, they have started with the service of providing locally Ghanaian drinks such as Bissap juice (sobolo), Asana, Lamugin and palm wine to their menu.<\/div>\n
<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n
<\/a><\/td>\n<\/tr>\n
Satisfying customer demands<\/td>\n<\/tr>\n<\/tbody>\n<\/table>\n
<\/div>\n
For an “exotic feel,”<\/em> they also provide fruit punches, alcoholic punches and alcoholic cocktails to their menu.<\/div>\n
