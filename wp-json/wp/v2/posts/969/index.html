WhatsApp, The online prominent instant messaging platform, has now made available an updated feature – The mute feature, for all Android users which has been used by a lot of Android users worldwide since its release. This feature initially permits users to disallow message notifications from groups and individual contacts for a period of time specifically to suit the user, now allows it without any time limit.<\/p>\n

This means users will not have to periodically revisit their mute decision to update it all the time. Until then, users could only mute notifications for 8 hours, 1 week, or a year in their WhatsApp settings, however, the recently launched option is now \u2018Always\u2019 and it is intended to replace the \u20181 year\u2019 option.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

<\/div>\n
<\/a><\/div>\n
The \u2018mute notification\u2019 choice can be activated by tapping on the three dots at the top right corner in a chat window which then will allow you to select a duration of your choice. WhatsApp does not notify affected contact or group of this decision when initiated. You should note that muting a group or private chat will not necessarily stop their messages from trooping in, it will only stop notifying you about the messages from the groups or people you have muted.<\/p>\n

Aside the general mute feature relating to messages, WhatsApp also enables users to mute their status updates. Muted statuses are usually hidden just below the list of permitted ones, but can be viewed at the user\u2019s will. This mute option is one of WhatsApp’s own way of helping users to create barriers or boundaries on whom ever they decide to interact with or not on the platform and also to avoid unnecessary spams and annoying notifications from these people or groups.<\/p>\n

However, the good thing about this is, it is only a modest restraint because the messages from muted contacts can still be found on the list of unread messages. Although a severe measure or action is the \u2018block\u2019 feature which is available on most social media platforms to prevent privacy invasion and spams. This avoids any form of dealings with contacts considered as threats but unfortunately, it cannot be used on WhatsApp groups. To compensate for that, the most helpful and effective way of preventing unwanted messages from those groups is to entirely exit the groups, which has been a lot of help to most people regarding invasion.<\/p>\n

Apart from this, users can change their privacy settings to allow only known or specific contacts to add them to groups they prefer. As at now, both the mute and block actions can be reversed if the need be. For now, the \u2018Always\u2019 mute option has been added to the list of terrific features on the prominent messaging app – Whatsapp.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n
