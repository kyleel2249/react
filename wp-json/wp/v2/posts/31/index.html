I like to go to work 30 minutes before most people start to arrive. I sit at my desk on the last floor and I take in the great view for 30 uninterrupted minutes. I look down on people as they go about their activities. Sometimes I think to myself, this must be how God watches down on us<\/i><\/a>. As I look at the day unfolding, my mind wanders and I think about a lot of things. Today, something caught my attention.<\/span><\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

From where I sit, I see a lot of offices. What I noticed, was the different ways in which young people walked into their workplaces. By young people, I mean people who outwardly look below 30. There are those who drive their own cars to work or are dropped off by their drivers. There are those who from Monday to Friday come in an Uber and there are those who are the trotro besties. Well obviously, there\u2019s nothing wrong with any of the means of transportation I\u2019ve mentioned because we all can\u2019t be the same. But I noticed how differently these people walk. Those that own cars or are dropped off by drivers or Uber walk out of the cars very confidently and feel somewhat important. Even when they are late, they seem not to be in a rush. They get out, take out their belongings from the car, lock their cars and even take a minute to straighten their clothes. When they meet their bosses or older staff on their way to the office, they remain calm and even engage in a chatty conversation as they enter. The trotro squad on the other hand just can\u2019t wait for the bus to stop for them to get down. They quickly get down and start to walk as fast as their legs will carry them trying also to balance their bags and dress up simultaneously. You see them sweating already and on days they are late, they walk like they are spies, making sure nobody in authority sees them.<\/p>\n
